I was born in Providence, Rhode Island, the smallest state in Union. I have a younger brother. My father was a postal clerk and my mother was a housewife.

I stayed pretty much within the boundaries of Rhode Island until I was 18 when I took a job in an airplane factory in Hartford, Connecticut. That did not last long because the Army wanted me.

After getting out of the Army I went to college on the GI Bill. I took my first programming course in 1968. It taught Fortran and I knew that I had found my calling. I still remember how I felt after the lesson on arrays - now I could do anything with a computer!

The programming course led to a job as an operator of an IBM 360 mainframe computer. I eventually worked as the third shift operator from midnight to 8am. During that time I had to run jobs that were too big to run when the time-sharing system was running. These "big" jobs required up to 512K of memory to run. The great thing about the job was that when all the jobs had run, I had this multi-million dollar computer to play with until the sun came up.

I still marvel at how fast computers have developed. These Android devices are much, much more powerful than that mainframe computer.
